About Shantivan

Deepak Nagargoje, along with his wife Kaveri, established Shantiwan.The villages of Taluka Shirur Kasar had been facing Drought conditions since the past few years. Arvi was one such village which was under this crisis. Consequently, many people in spite of having land couldn’ t cultivate enough crop to generate income vital for sustaining themselves. Hence, a huge rate of outward seasonal migration towards western Maharashtra and Karnataka is witnessed, for finding employment in sugarcane industry as sugarcane cutters. Because of this, the educationof children of these farmers was adversely affected, as they migrate back and forth with their parents they were left to be out of school when they come back into the village. The welfare of these children was the primary focus of Shatiwan. Which over time also gave shelter to children of “ Tamasha” workers, Red light workers, orphans and any child in the need of care. This problem has been resolved to an extent in Arvi Village and the villages surrounding Arvi but it still plagues much of Marathwada.

Shantiwan aims to address the issue at the grass root level, which is the lack of schools which support the aim of education at its entirety. Shantiwan runs a residential school teaching classes 1 to 10. It caters to 784 students out of which about 200 stay on campus (This number goes up to 350 during the months of migration). Being a residential school the students are provided with an all-round development. The primary needs of food, clothing and shelter are taken care of by Shantiwan. The education provided at Shantivan is free of cost, along with the nutritious food, uniforms and the other school supplies for the students. Rest of the students are brought in every morning by the school buses. Nagargoje believes that education is a path towards leading an informed life.

Keeping all this in mind, along with standard schooling Deepak has designed the model of Shantiwan in a competent way. The model is successful in addressing maximum issues with single intervention. It promotes the overall Human and Cognitive Development of the Students.The everyday routine of the children mirrors this observation. A typical day at Shantiwan starts with every student performing Yoga along with an instructor. Students areencouraged to meditate for ten minutes after Yoga.This is also reflected in the fact that the entire batch of students who appeared for the Maharashtra State Board Exam secured more than 80%.

Having said that, the backgrounds of the children at shantivan are not to be forgotten. The children come from socially crippling circumstances. Many children have lost their parents to accidents that occur in sugarcane farms. Then there are children who have been abandoned by their parents (Sex worker’ s and Tamasha worker’ s children) who are neglected in spite of having a living parent. Till the time these children are brought into Shantiwan they have gone through numerous incidences of exploitation and neglect sometimes even on the hands of their own relatives. As a result these children are highly depressed and low on self-esteem. Hence the role of Shantiwan become more significant in the sense that it provides much more than a normal school. Each student is taken care of and nurtured by Kaveri Ma’am at Shantiwan.

Deepak Nagargoje follows and promotes the principles of living a life enriched with good ethics and honor irrespective of one’ s background. This is well reflected in the bright eyes and vibrant faces residing in Shantiwan. The premise is a beautiful place to stay and study in for a child. Life at Shantiwan is a life lived in a very close harmony with nature.The plants and trees in the campus are watered using the treated water in channelized towards watering the plants in the premise.

We often wonder how this work progresses through so difficult situations. Many unseen hands keep coming forward to strengthen our social workers efforts.
